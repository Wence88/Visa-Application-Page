About Ilawe Kingdom

Ilawe Ekiti is the headquarter of Ekiti South West Local Government, Nigeria.

With an estimated population of over 200,000, Ilawe-Ekiti ranks as one of the top and popular cities in Ekiti State.

Yoruba Language, one of the most spoken languge in the world is the language of Ilawe kingdom. Yoruba is the predominant language in Ekiti State.

The Yoruba culture, including its rich traditions, festivals, and art, is an essential part of the region's identity.

Agriculture is a major part of the economy in Ekiti State. The state is known for the cultivation of crops such as cocoa, yam, cassava, and plantains.

The people of Ekiti State, including Ilawe Ekiti, are generally known for their warmth and hospitality. Visitors often note the friendliness of the locals.

Locality

Ilawe Ekiti is divided into 8 different Quarters: Okebedo, Okeemo, Irorin, Aaye, Okepa, Okeloye, Iro, and Adin.

Ruling Monarch

The current King of Ilawe is His Royal Majesty Oba Adebanji Ajibade Alabi (Afuntande 1).
He ascended to the throne on 21 April 2012, taking over from Oba Joseph Ademileka.

Fun Facts

  • Ekiti State has the highest number of professor in Nigeria, Ilawe has a great amount from these.
  • Ilawe Ekiti is one of the highest producers of Cocoa and Plantains in Nigeria.
  • Ilawe is one of the highest consumers of alcohol in Nigeria and the highest consumer in southwestern Nigeria.
  • Ilawe is one of the largest cities in Ekiti State.
